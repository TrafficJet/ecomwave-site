# How to Start a Shopify Store in 2024 (Step-by-Step)

Starting an online store doesn't require a computer science degree or a massive bank account. Shopify powers over 4.4 million active stores worldwide, and the platform handles hosting, security, and checkout so you can focus on finding customers and making sales (Source: Shopify, 2024). This guide walks you through every step — from signing up to getting your first sale — with real costs, real timelines, and zero fluff.

## What You Need Before You Build Your Store

Before you touch the Shopify admin, get four things ready: a business idea, a specific niche, a basic monthly budget of $39 for the Shopify Basic plan (as of 2024), and a dedicated business email address. Having a niche — say, eco-friendly pet products instead of just "pet supplies" — makes marketing dramatically easier and cheaper because you can target a well-defined audience with specific messaging instead of competing with Amazon on broad terms.

Setting up a legal business entity like an LLC or sole proprietorship is recommended but not required on day one. Shopify lets you sign up with just a personal email and start building immediately with a 3-day free trial — no credit card needed.

Set realistic expectations here. Your first sale typically takes 2–8 weeks with active marketing. **Example:** Sarah, a first-time seller who launched a handmade candle store in March 2024, made her first sale on day 12 after posting consistently on TikTok and running a small Meta ad campaign. She spent under $600 total to get to that point — $39 for Shopify, roughly $200 on initial candle-making supplies, and $350 on ads.

## Step 2 — Pick a Fast, Mobile-First Shopify Theme

Your theme controls how your store looks, feels, and performs. Shopify offers 12+ free themes — including Dawn, Craft, and Sense — plus over 100 paid themes ranging from $100 to $380 in the Shopify Theme Store (as of 2024).

**Dawn is the go-to recommendation for beginners.** It loads fast (typically under 2 seconds on a 4G connection), looks clean, and is fully mobile-optimized. That last point matters: over 60% of United States ecommerce traffic now comes from mobile devices (Source: Statista, 2024). According to Baymard Institute's mobile UX research, poor mobile experience is a leading driver of cart abandonment — so always check your store's mobile preview before publishing any changes.

Inside the Theme Editor (**Online Store > Themes > Customize**), you can change colors, fonts, your logo, and rearrange homepage sections using a drag-and-drop interface. No coding required. For a quick logo, Canva's free logo templates work well — most store owners create a solid first logo in under 10 minutes.

One limitation to know: free themes offer fewer customizable sections and layout options than paid themes. If you need advanced features like mega menus, multi-column product tabs, or built-in upsell modules, a paid theme is typically worth the investment.

## Step 3 — Add Products That Convert

Navigate to **Products > Add Product** in your Shopify admin. Each product listing has several fields: title, description, images, price, compare-at price (for showing strikethrough discounts), SKU (stock keeping unit — a unique identifier you assign to each product), and inventory count. Fill in every field — incomplete listings look unprofessional and hurt conversion rates.

### Product Photos

Product photos make or break your sales. Baymard Institute research shows that insufficient product imagery is among the top reasons shoppers abandon product pages (Source: Baymard Institute, 2023). Shoot on a plain white background, capture at least 3–4 angles, and include one lifestyle shot showing the product in use. A smartphone with good natural lighting is enough to start.

### Product Descriptions

Write descriptions that focus on benefits, not just features. Instead of "16 oz stainless steel tumbler," try "Keeps your coffee hot for 6 hours — the 16 oz stainless steel tumbler that fits in standard cup holders." Our guide on [how to write product descriptions](/how-to-write-product-descriptions) covers this in depth.

### Variants and Product Types

If your product comes in variants — like size or color — Shopify lets you set different prices, SKUs, and inventory levels for each variant under the "Variants" section of the product page. The platform supports physical products, digital downloads (like PDFs or courses), and services.

**Dropshipping route:** If you don't want to hold inventory, apps like DSers connect your store directly to AliExpress suppliers. AutoDS offers more automation for order fulfillment. The tradeoff: you sacrifice control over shipping speed and product quality, and margins tend to be thinner. Our [Shopify dropshipping guide](/shopify-dropshipping-guide) walks through that entire model.

## Step 4 — Configure Shipping to Protect Margins and Boost AOV

Go to **Settings > Shipping and Delivery** to configure how products reach your customers. You'll see two main options:

- **Flat rate shipping** (e.g., $5.99 for all orders) — simple and predictable for customers

- **Calculated shipping** — pulls real-time rates from carriers like USPS and UPS, which works well for stores with products that vary significantly in weight

Calculated rates require the Basic plan or higher.

A proven strategy to raise your average order value (AOV — the average dollar amount per transaction): offer free shipping above a threshold, like "Free shipping on orders over $50." Stores using this tactic typically see AOV increases of 15–30% (Source: Shopify Blog, 2023). Shopify Shipping gives you built-in discounted rates from USPS, UPS, and DHL — often 40–60% cheaper than retail rates.

**Example:** A pet accessories store owner tested a $45 free shipping threshold and saw her AOV climb from $32 to $48 within the first month. She absorbed $5–$7 in average shipping costs per order but increased profit per order overall.

For taxes, go to **Settings > Taxes and Duties**. Shopify automatically calculates and collects sales tax in most US states based on your nexus (the legal threshold of business presence that obligates you to collect tax in a given state). The platform handles the math, but consult a tax professional to confirm where you actually have nexus obligations, especially if you store inventory in multiple states or use fulfillment centers like Amazon FBA.

## Step 5 — Set Up Payments to Maximize Checkout Conversion

Shopify Payments is the default payment processor and the smartest choice for most United States–based stores. It eliminates the extra transaction fee, supports all major credit cards, and integrates with Shop Pay — Shopify's accelerated checkout option. Shopify reports that Shop Pay increases checkout conversion by up to 50% compared to standard guest checkout (Source: Shopify, 2024). That figure reflects the advantage of stored payment and shipping info, so results vary depending on how much of your traffic consists of returning shoppers.

To activate it, go to **Settings > Payments > Complete account setup**. Enter your banking details, business information, and tax ID (EIN or SSN). The whole process takes about 10 minutes.

If you prefer PayPal or Stripe as alternative gateways, know that Shopify charges an additional 0.5%–2% transaction fee on top of the gateway's own processing costs. On a store doing $10,000/month, that's an extra $50–$200 in fees. Enable Shop Pay Installments and consider adding Afterpay or Klarna for buy-now-pay-later (BNPL) options — these can reduce cart abandonment, especially on items priced above $50.

One limitation: Shopify Payments isn't available in every country and can hold payouts for new stores during an initial review period, typically 1–3 business days but occasionally longer.

## Step 6 — Add Essential Pages and Legal Policies That Build Trust

Every credible store needs three core pages: **About Us**, **Contact**, and **FAQ**. The About Us page builds trust by telling your brand story — Nielsen research consistently shows that consumers prefer buying from brands whose values and story they connect with (Source: Nielsen, 2023). The Contact page should include a working email and, ideally, a response time commitment (e.g., "We reply within 24 hours").

Shopify auto-generates legal page templates under **Settings > Policies**: Privacy Policy, Refund Policy, Terms of Service, and Shipping Policy. Customize these templates with your specific details — don't just publish the defaults as-is. Payment processors like Shopify Payments and PayPal require these pages to be active on your store, and Baymard Institute research identifies missing return/refund information as a top contributor to checkout abandonment (Source: Baymard Institute, 2023).

Once your pages are created, add them to your site's navigation. Go to **Online Store > Navigation** and add pages to your header menu (About, Shop, Contact) and footer menu (policies, FAQ). Clean navigation keeps bounce rates low and helps customers find what they need without hunting.

## Step 7 — Connect a Custom Domain to Signal Legitimacy

A custom domain costs about $14–$20/year for a .com purchased directly through Shopify (**Settings > Domains > Buy new domain**), or you can transfer one from registrars like GoDaddy, Namecheap, or Cloudflare Registrar.

Removing the default yourstore.myshopify.com URL matters more than you might think. A branded .com domain signals legitimacy — customers are significantly less likely to enter credit card information on a URL that screams "I just started this yesterday." Merchants who switch from a myshopify.com URL to a custom domain often report immediate improvements in customer trust during checkout.

For a professional email to match (hello@yourstore.com), Google Workspace runs $7.20/month (as of 2024) and integrates easily. Zoho Mail offers a free tier for up to 5 users if you're keeping costs minimal.

## Step 8 — Install Only the Apps You Actually Need at Launch

The Shopify App Store has over 8,000 apps, but restraint is critical. Install 3–5 apps maximum at launch. Every app adds JavaScript to your store's front end, and too many will slow down page load times. Google's Core Web Vitals — the page experience metrics that influence search rankings — penalize slow-loading stores (Source: Google Search Central, 2023).

**Recommended starter apps (all have free tiers as of 2024):**

- **Shopify Email** — built-in email marketing; up to 10,000 emails/month free

- **Judge.me** — collect and display product reviews (social proof is one of the strongest conversion drivers for new stores)

- **Klaviyo** — advanced email marketing and automations for when you outgrow Shopify Email

- **Google Analytics 4** — connect via Shopify's built-in integration panel under **Online Store > Preferences**

For SEO, use Shopify's built-in meta title and description fields and add alt text (a brief text description of an image, used by search engines and screen readers) to every product image. An app like Plug In SEO can flag technical issues, but manual optimization — writing unique meta descriptions, structuring headings properly, and compressing images — is more effective long-term.

**A common mistake:** Merchants install 10–15 apps in the first week, then wonder why their store takes 6+ seconds to load. If you later uninstall an app, check your theme code — some apps leave behind residual code snippets that continue affecting performance.

## Step 9 — Launch Your Store and Drive Your First Traffic

Before you flip the switch, run through this pre-launch checklist:

- [ ] Place a real test order (Shopify has a Bogus Gateway under **Settings > Payments** for this)

- [ ] Check every page on mobile — tap every button, fill out every form

- [ ] Verify that order confirmation emails send correctly

- [ ] Review your checkout flow from cart to thank-you page

- [ ] Confirm shipping rates display accurately for your target regions

When everything looks solid, remove password protection by going to **Online Store > Preferences** and disabling the password page. Your store is now live.

### Free Traffic Channels

- **TikTok organic** — short-form product videos are typically the fastest free traffic source for new brands in 2024. TikTok Shop integration lets customers buy without leaving the app.

- **Instagram Reels and Pinterest** — repurpose your TikTok content here to maximize reach without creating new assets.

- **SEO blog content** — publish 2–3 articles targeting keywords your customers search for. This is a slower channel (expect 2–6 months for meaningful organic traffic) but compounds over time.

### Paid Traffic Channels

Meta ads (Facebook/Instagram via Meta Business Suite) and Google Shopping are the two highest-converting paid channels for United States ecommerce stores in most categories. Start with a $10–$20/day Meta ad test budget and run it for 5–7 days before making changes — the algorithm needs data to optimize.

Influencer micro-partnerships in the $100–$500 range can also drive targeted traffic at launch. Merchants who find micro-influencers (1,000–50,000 followers) in their specific niche often see better ROI (return on investment) than broad paid ads, especially on TikTok and Instagram.

**One honest caveat:** Paid ads require testing and iteration. Most new store owners don't find a profitable ad on the first attempt. Budget for 2–4 weeks of testing before expecting consistent returns.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How long does it take to set up a Shopify store?

Most people can set up a basic Shopify store in one to three days. Building it out with products, policies, and branding typically takes a full weekend of focused work.

### Do I need an LLC to start a Shopify store?

No. You can start as a sole proprietor with just your Social Security Number. Many sellers form an LLC later once they're generating consistent revenue — primarily for liability protection and to separate personal and business finances.

### Can I start a Shopify store with no money?

Shopify offers a 3-day free trial, and the cheapest full plan is $39/month. The Starter plan runs $5/month but doesn't include a full storefront. Dropshipping instead of buying inventory upfront significantly reduces startup costs, though it doesn't eliminate them entirely.

### Is Shopify good for beginners?

Shopify is widely considered the most beginner-friendly ecommerce platform in the United States. It handles hosting, security, SSL certificates, and most technical details. The tradeoff compared to open-source platforms like WooCommerce is less customization flexibility and ongoing monthly costs rather than self-hosting.

### How do I get my first sale on Shopify?

The fastest path to a first sale is usually paid social ads (Meta or TikTok), posting organic short-form video content, or reaching out directly to your personal network. Most merchants who actively market their store see their first sale within 2–8 weeks.

### What is the best Shopify plan for a new store?

The Basic plan at $39/month is the right starting point for most new stores. It includes unlimited products, Shopify Payments, discount codes, and basic reports. Upgrade to the Shopify plan ($105/month) when you need professional reports or are processing enough volume to benefit from lower card rates.

### Does Shopify take a percentage of my sales?

If you use Shopify Payments, there are no additional transaction fees — only standard credit card processing rates (2.9% + 30¢ on the Basic plan as of 2024). Using a third-party payment gateway adds a 0.5%–2% transaction fee on top of the gateway's own processing costs.

# Online Store Checklist: Launch Right in 2026

Launching an ecommerce store without a checklist is like flying a plane without a pre-flight inspection. You might get off the ground, but you're gambling with every dollar you've spent getting there. This step-by-step online store checklist covers eight core areas — from domain setup to your first 30 days of live sales — so you can launch with confidence and avoid costly mistakes.

---

## Why You Need an Online Store Checklist Before Launch

Over 20% of new ecommerce stores lose revenue in their first week because of preventable setup errors like broken checkout flows, missing payment gateways, or misconfigured shipping rates (Baymard Institute, 2026). Those aren't minor inconveniences. They mean abandoned carts, failed payments, and lost customer trust that's hard to rebuild.

Skipping steps also creates long-term problems. A store that launches without proper SEO setup can face Google indexing delays or ranking penalties. A checkout page missing SSL — the security protocol that encrypts data between a browser and your server — will trigger browser warnings that scare buyers away immediately.

Merchants who treat launch day as "figure it out as we go" typically spend their first two weeks firefighting instead of marketing. This guide covers eight core areas: domain, product pages, payments, shipping, legal, SEO, pre-launch testing, and post-launch monitoring.

## Domain, Hosting, and Platform Setup

Your domain name is the first thing customers see. Pick one that matches your brand and is easy to spell. If you can naturally include your primary keyword — like "organicpetfood.com" — that's a small SEO bonus. But never force it at the expense of brand clarity.

Platform choice depends on your technical comfort level and budget. **Shopify** (Basic plan at $39/month as of 2025) is the fastest path for sellers who want everything managed. **WooCommerce** is free but requires your own hosting and more hands-on maintenance. **BigCommerce** (Standard plan at $39/month as of 2025) sits between the two, offering strong built-in features without plugins (Shopify.com; BigCommerce.com). For a deeper breakdown, check out our [ecommerce platform comparison](/ecommerce-platform-comparison).

Once you've chosen a platform, connect your custom domain and confirm DNS propagation is complete — this can take up to 48 hours. Verify your hosting meets current Core Web Vitals targets: Largest Contentful Paint (LCP) under 2.5 seconds and Interaction to Next Paint (INP) under 200 milliseconds (Google Web Vitals documentation, 2024). Then enable your **SSL certificate**. Every major platform includes one, but check that the padlock icon appears on every page. Google uses SSL as a ranking factor, and browsers flag non-SSL sites as "Not Secure."

**Example:** When DTC olive oil brand Graza launched their store, they chose Shopify and registered a short, brandable domain. Their site loaded in under 1.8 seconds from day one because they compressed and resized images before uploading. Many first-time merchants skip this step, then discover their 4MB product photos are tanking their page speed scores.

## Product Pages and Catalog Checklist

Your product pages do the selling. Treat each one like a dedicated landing page. Write unique product titles and descriptions for every item — copying manufacturer text creates duplicate content that Google typically filters out of search results. Need help with this? Our [product description writing guide](/how-to-write-product-descriptions) walks you through it.

Add descriptive alt text to every product image. Alt text is the written description embedded in an image's HTML that screen readers announce to visually impaired users. It also gives Google more context about your products for image search rankings.

Set accurate inventory counts and assign SKUs (stock keeping units — unique identifiers for each product) to every item so your stock tracking works from day one. If you sell items with variants — size, color, material — make sure each variant has its own pricing, image, and inventory count.

Add **Product schema markup** (structured data code that helps search engines understand your page content), including Review and Price fields, so Google can display rich results with star ratings and pricing directly in search. Over 58% of US ecommerce traffic now comes from mobile devices (Statista, 2025), so test every product page on your phone. Check that images load quickly, buttons are tap-friendly (at least 48×48 pixels), and the "Add to Cart" button is visible without scrolling sideways.

**Example:** Merchants who sell apparel often launch with a single image per color variant, then wonder why return rates are high. Allbirds, by contrast, shows each colorway with its own set of lifestyle and detail shots. This reduces "it looked different online" returns and builds buyer confidence before purchase.

## Payment Gateway and Checkout Checklist

Offer at least two payment methods at launch. **Stripe** handles credit and debit cards at 2.9% + $0.30 per transaction, and **PayPal** gives customers a trusted alternative they already use (Stripe.com, as of 2025). Together, those two cover the vast majority of US shoppers.

Add a buy-now-pay-later (BNPL) option like **Klarna** or **Afterpay**. BNPL — which lets customers split purchases into installment payments — grew 34% year-over-year in adoption among US consumers, especially for orders over $75 (PYMNTS.com, 2024). Before you go live, run a real end-to-end test transaction: enter a card, complete checkout, and confirm the order shows up in your dashboard. Verify that your checkout is SSL-secured and PCI compliant. PCI compliance is a set of security standards for handling credit card data — all major platforms handle it, but check your settings to confirm nothing is misconfigured.

Set up abandoned cart recovery emails through **Klaviyo** or **Mailchimp**. Abandoned cart emails recover roughly 7–8% of lost sales when sent within one hour of abandonment (Klaviyo, 2024). See our [abandoned cart email examples](/abandoned-cart-email-examples) for templates that convert.

Also, enable guest checkout. Forcing account creation is one of the top reasons shoppers abandon at the payment step. Baymard Institute's checkout usability research (2024) found that 26% of US online shoppers have abandoned an order specifically because a site required them to create an account.

**Example:** Home goods brand Caraway added Afterpay to their checkout after noticing a high drop-off rate on products priced above $100. Their conversion rate on those items reportedly jumped significantly within the first month. This pattern is common among merchants selling in the $75–$200 range — BNPL removes the psychological friction of a larger single payment.

## Shipping and Fulfillment Checklist

Configure your shipping zones starting with domestic US. If you plan to ship internationally, set those zones up separately with their own rates and estimated delivery windows. Connect carrier accounts for **USPS**, **UPS**, and **FedEx** to offer real-time rate quoting at checkout — this prevents you from undercharging or overcharging on shipping.

Set a free shipping threshold. A free shipping minimum — for example, "Free shipping on orders over $50" — typically increases average order value by 15–20% (National Retail Federation, 2025). Write a clear shipping policy page that lists estimated delivery times, carrier options, and how customers can track orders. Use our [shipping policy template](/shipping-policy-template) as a starting point.

Decide on your fulfillment model before launch day: are you packing and shipping orders yourself, using a third-party logistics provider (3PL — a company that warehouses and ships products on your behalf), or dropshipping? Each model has different lead times and cost structures. Switching mid-launch creates chaos.

Merchants who self-fulfill often underestimate packing time during a launch spike. If you expect more than 20 orders per day in your first week, have a fulfillment backup plan ready. As a final check, generate test shipping labels through your platform to confirm the carrier integration actually works.

## Legal Pages and Compliance Checklist

You need three legal pages at minimum: a **Privacy Policy**, **Terms of Service**, and a **Return/Refund Policy**. If you sell to California residents — and you almost certainly will — your Privacy Policy must meet **CCPA** (California Consumer Privacy Act) requirements. That includes disclosing what personal data you collect and giving customers the right to opt out of data sales (California Attorney General's office, 2024). Grab our [return policy template](/return-policy-template) to get started.

Display a cookie consent banner if you're collecting analytics data. This is required under **GDPR** (General Data Protection Regulation) for European visitors who land on your site. Failing to show one can result in fines up to €20 million (GDPR.eu). Even if you only target US customers, international visitors will find you through search.

Cover ADA accessibility basics: maintain a minimum 4.5:1 color contrast ratio, ensure all interactive elements are keyboard-navigable, and confirm all images have alt text (you already did this on product pages). ADA-related web accessibility lawsuits in the US exceeded 4,000 filings in 2023 (UsableNet, 2024), so this is a legal risk worth addressing — though specific standards for ecommerce sites are still evolving.

Include your business name, contact email, phone number, and physical address in your site footer. This builds trust and is required in many states for online businesses.

## SEO and Analytics Checklist

Install **Google Analytics 4** (GA4) and verify that data is flowing correctly before launch — not after. In Shopify, navigate to **Online Store > Preferences** and paste your GA4 Measurement ID (format: G-XXXXXXXXXX) into the Google Analytics field. Set up ecommerce event tracking so you can see add-to-cart actions, checkout starts, and completed purchases. Our [GA4 setup guide for ecommerce](/google-analytics-4-setup-ecommerce) walks you through every step.

Connect **Google Search Console**, verify your domain, and submit your XML sitemap. This tells Google your site exists and speeds up initial crawling. Most new sites see their first indexed pages within 2–7 days of sitemap submission, but full indexing can take several weeks.

Install the **Meta Pixel** if you plan to run Facebook or Instagram ads. Retargeting warm visitors — people who've already browsed your products — typically costs 50–75% less per acquisition than cold traffic campaigns.

Write unique meta titles (under 60 characters) and meta descriptions (under 155 characters) for your homepage, every category page, and every product page. Duplicate or missing meta tags are one of the most common SEO mistakes at launch. Check your page speed using Google PageSpeed Insights and target an LCP under 2.5 seconds. If you're migrating from another domain or platform, set up 301 redirects for every old URL to prevent 404 errors and preserve any existing link authority. For a complete SEO strategy, see our [ecommerce SEO guide](/ecommerce-seo-guide).

**Example:** When kitchenware brand Our Place migrated to a new site structure, they mapped over 300 old URLs to new ones using 301 redirects. This preserved their organic traffic through the transition. Merchants who skip this step often watch their search traffic drop 40–60% overnight.

## Pre-Launch Testing Checklist

Test every form on your site: contact forms, newsletter signups, and — most critically — the checkout form. Fill them out with real information and confirm submissions arrive where they should: your inbox, your email platform, your order dashboard.

Click every navigation link and every CTA button on both desktop and mobile. Broken links on launch day look amateur and cost you sales. Place a real test order using an actual payment method, then process a refund on that order to confirm both flows work correctly.

Check that all automated emails fire properly: order confirmations, shipping update notifications, and refund receipts. Open each one and verify the formatting, links, and brand details are correct. Merchants who skip this often discover their transactional emails still contain placeholder text or default branding. That's a poor first impression for early customers.

Review your site on Chrome, Safari, and Firefox. Rendering differences between browsers can hide layout bugs you'd never catch on your primary browser alone. Pay particular attention to checkout page styling and form field behavior across browsers.

Run a **Google Lighthouse audit** (built into Chrome DevTools — right-click any page, select "Inspect," then click the "Lighthouse" tab) and address any critical issues flagged under Performance, Accessibility, and SEO categories. Aim for scores above 90 in each category. Fix anything in red before going live.

## Post-Launch Checklist: First 30 Days

Your launch isn't the finish line — it's the starting point for optimization. Monitor **Google Search Console** weekly for crawl errors, 404 pages, and indexing issues. Fix problems within 48 hours to avoid compounding SEO damage.

Set up a post-purchase email sequence that requests product reviews 7–10 days after delivery. Reviews build social proof and feed your Product schema for richer Google results. Launch your first email campaign through **Klaviyo** or **Mailchimp** announcing your store is open. Your email list is typically your highest-converting channel from day one — email marketing generates an average of $36 for every $1 spent across ecommerce (Litmus, 2023).

Install a free heatmap tool like **Hotjar** or **Microsoft Clarity** to visualize where users click, scroll, and rage-click (rapidly clicking the same element out of frustration). This data reveals UX problems that analytics alone can't show. Review your GA4 funnel reports to identify exactly where users drop off — whether it's on product pages, during cart review, or at the payment step.

Schedule a formal 30-day audit where you review your overall conversion rate, top exit pages, and average order value. Then build your improvement plan from that data. A healthy benchmark for new ecommerce stores is a 1.5–2.5% conversion rate in the first month (Kibo Commerce, 2024). If you're below 1%, your testing checklist likely missed a friction point.

**Example:** After launching, DTC skincare brand Topicals used Microsoft Clarity heatmaps to discover that 40% of mobile users never scrolled past the hero image on their homepage. They moved their bestseller carousel above the fold and saw a 17% increase in product page visits within two weeks. That kind of insight is nearly impossible to get from GA4 alone — which is why pairing analytics with session-recording tools is standard practice among high-performing ecommerce teams.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is an online store checklist?
An online store checklist is a step-by-step list covering every task you need to complete before and after launching an ecommerce site — from domain setup and payment gateways to SEO and legal pages.

### How long does it take to launch an online store in 2026?
Most sellers using a platform like Shopify or BigCommerce can launch a basic store in 1–2 weeks if they follow a structured checklist. Custom builds on WooCommerce or headless platforms typically take 4–8 weeks, depending on design complexity and integrations.

### Do I need an SSL certificate for my online store?
Yes. SSL is required for secure checkout, customer trust, and Google ranking. All major ecommerce platforms include SSL, but confirm it's active on every page — including your blog and policy pages, not just checkout — before launch.

### What payment methods should I offer at launch?
At minimum, offer credit and debit card payments via Stripe, plus PayPal. Adding a buy-now-pay-later option like Klarna or Afterpay can reduce cart abandonment, especially for items priced over $75. One limitation to note: BNPL providers charge merchants a higher transaction fee (typically 4–6%) compared to standard card processing.

### What legal pages does my online store need?
You need a Privacy Policy, Terms of Service, and a Return/Refund Policy at minimum. If you sell to California residents, your Privacy Policy must comply with CCPA requirements. Consulting an attorney familiar with ecommerce law is advisable if your store handles sensitive data or operates in regulated product categories.

### How do I check if my online store is ready to launch?
Run through a pre-launch checklist: place a test order, click every link, check your mobile layout, verify all automated emails send correctly, and run a Google Lighthouse audit to catch speed or accessibility issues before customers do. If possible, have two or three people outside your team complete a test purchase and report any friction points.

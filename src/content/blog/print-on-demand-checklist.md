# Print on Demand Checklist: Launch Without Mistakes

Starting your first print on demand store means making dozens of small decisions. Miss one — like skipping a trademark search or forgetting sales tax setup — and you're looking at refunds, account suspensions, or weeks of wasted work. This print on demand checklist walks you through every step, from niche research to your first sale, so you launch without the common mistakes.

## What Is a Print on Demand Checklist and Why You Need One

Print on demand (POD) is a fulfillment model where you sell custom-designed products — t-shirts, mugs, posters, tote bags — without holding inventory. A customer places an order. Your supplier prints the design and ships it directly to the buyer. You never touch the product.

Skipping steps causes real problems. Sellers who rush past niche validation end up with listings nobody searches for. Those who skip sample orders get hit with bad reviews when print quality doesn't match their mockups. A 2025 eRank Seller Report found that 34% of Etsy shop closures within the first year were tied to preventable issues — policy violations, pricing mistakes.

This checklist is built for new sellers, side hustlers, and small brands launching their first POD store. It covers everything from design creation through your first confirmed sale. Just actionable steps.

## Step 1: Validate Your Niche Before You Design Anything

Your designs don't matter if nobody searches for what you sell. Before you open Canva or Adobe Illustrator, confirm that real demand exists.

Start with Google Trends and Etsy search autocomplete. Type "funny retirement shirt" into Etsy's search bar — if you see a dozen autocomplete suggestions, that signals active buyer interest. Cross-reference by checking Amazon Merch on Demand bestseller ranks. Products consistently ranked in the top 100 of a category show sustained demand, not a one-time spike.

Avoid oversaturated niches with no specific angle. "Dog mom" t-shirts and generic "nurse life" designs have thousands of competitors. Target a specific audience instead — left-handed teachers, disc golfers over 50, retired military pilots. These micro-niches have less competition and more passionate buyers. Broad-niche sellers often find themselves buried on page 20 of search results, competing on price against sellers with years of review history.

Use a keyword tool like eRank or Marmalead to estimate monthly search volume. Aim for niches with at least 1,000 monthly searches on Etsy but fewer than 10,000 competing listings.

**Real-world example:** A seller targeting "retired firefighter dad" shirts found 2,400 monthly Etsy searches with only 800 competing listings — a much better ratio than "firefighter shirt" with 40,000+ competitors.

The FTC requires that any income claims in marketing content be truthful and substantiated. If you're creating content around your niche research, don't promise buyers or followers specific earnings without evidence.

## Step 2: Choose the Right Print on Demand Supplier

Your supplier controls product quality, shipping speed, and the return experience. Choose poorly and you'll spend more time apologizing to customers than making sales.

Here's how the three major US-friendly suppliers compare as of 2026:

| Feature | Printful | Printify | Gelato |
|---|---|---|---|
| Base cost (unisex tee) | ~$12.95 | ~$7.50–$12.00 (varies by provider) | ~$10.50 |
| US production time | 2–5 business days | 2–5 business days | 2–5 business days |
| US print facilities | Yes (Charlotte, NC; Dallas, TX) | Yes (network of providers) | Yes (multiple US nodes) |
| Shopify/Etsy/WooCommerce integration | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| Reprint policy | Free reprints for defects | Depends on print provider | Free reprints for defects |

(Source: Printful, Printify, and Gelato pricing pages, as of 2026)

**The tradeoff:** Printful offers more consistent quality because it owns its production facilities, but its higher base cost cuts into margins. Printify's marketplace model gives you lower base costs, but quality varies between print providers — you need to test specific providers individually. Gelato is strong for international orders with local production in 30+ countries, though its US catalog is smaller than Printful's.

Before you list a single product, order samples from at least two suppliers. Compare the actual print against the mockup — colors often look different on screen versus on fabric. Photograph your samples and use those real photos alongside mockups in your listings. Baymard Institute's product page UX research (2024) found that shoppers rate real product photography as significantly more trustworthy than digital renders alone.

Check that your supplier ships via USPS or UPS with tracking included. Review their return and reprint policies in writing so you know exactly what's covered when a customer complains. For a deeper breakdown, see our [Printful vs. Printify comparison](/printful-vs-printify-comparison).

## Step 3: Create Print-Ready Artwork That Sells

The top technical mistake new POD sellers make is uploading low-resolution designs. DPI (dots per inch) measures print resolution. Every design file should be at minimum 300 DPI. Anything lower looks blurry and pixelated once printed — especially on larger products like sweatshirts and posters.

Each product type has its own template with bleed areas (the extra space beyond the trim line that prevents white edges), safe zones (where critical design elements should stay), and trim lines (where the final cut happens). A design that works on a mug won't automatically work on a 16x20 poster. Download the specific template from your supplier's site and design within those boundaries.

Adobe Illustrator is ideal for vector-based artwork that scales to any size without losing quality. Canva Pro works well for typography-heavy designs and quick template-based layouts, especially if you're not a trained designer. Both are viable. The key is exporting at the correct DPI and dimensions for your chosen product.

**Protect yourself legally.** Run every phrase and design concept through a trademark search at USPTO.gov before you list it. Phrases like "Let's Go Brandon" and "Girl Boss" have active trademark registrations. Using them without a license can get your shop shut down. Also avoid copyrighted clip art, unlicensed fonts, and fan art of any kind.

**Real-world example:** A seller created a design reading "Fueled By Spite and Coffee." Before listing, she searched USPTO.gov and found no active trademark. She listed the shirt — it became her top seller with 200+ units in 60 days. A competitor who used a trademarked phrase in the same niche had their entire Etsy shop suspended.

Test each design on at least three colorways — black, white, and one mid-tone — and check how it looks across sizes from S to 3XL. What looks great on a medium tee often appears stretched or oddly placed on a 3XL. That leads to returns and negative reviews.

## Step 4: Set Up Your Store the Right Way

Pick your sales channel based on where your audience already shops. Shopify gives you full control over branding and customer data, with plans starting at $39/month as of 2026 (Source: Shopify Pricing Page, 2026). Etsy gives you immediate access to 96 million active buyers but takes a larger cut per transaction through listing fees, transaction fees, and payment processing fees (Source: Etsy Q4 Earnings Report, 2025). Many successful sellers run both — Etsy for discovery, Shopify for repeat customers. For a detailed comparison, check out our guide on [Shopify vs. Etsy for print on demand](/shopify-vs-etsy-for-print-on-demand).

**The tradeoff:** Shopify requires you to drive all your own traffic, which means higher upfront marketing costs. Etsy provides built-in traffic but less control over your brand and customer relationships. Neither is universally better. Your choice depends on whether you have an existing audience or need marketplace visibility.

Write a clear shop bio that tells buyers who you are and what your brand stands for. Add a real photo or professional logo — shops with a profile image typically get more clicks. Set up payment processing through Shopify Payments or Etsy Payments. Confirm you're collecting US sales tax in every state where you have nexus — a legal connection to a state that triggers tax obligations. Tax rules shifted for several states in 2025, so verify current requirements through your state's Department of Revenue site or consult a tax professional.

You need three policies before your first listing goes live:

1. **Shipping policy** with realistic delivery windows (production time + transit time)
2. **Return policy** that aligns with your supplier's reprint policy
3. **Production time notice** so buyers know items are made to order

Connect your POD supplier app — Printful, Printify, or Gelato — and sync your products. On Shopify, go to **Settings → Apps and sales channels** to manage your integration.

Install Google Analytics (via **Online Store → Preferences** in Shopify) and Meta Pixel on day one so you start collecting visitor data immediately. Set up Google Merchant Center to get your products into free Google Shopping listings. This is organic traffic most new sellers overlook entirely.

## Step 5: Price Your Products for Profit, Not Just Sales

Use this formula every time you set a price:

**Base cost + platform fees + desired profit margin = list price**

For example, if your Printify base cost for a t-shirt is $9.50, Etsy charges a 6.5% transaction fee plus $0.20 listing fee, and you want $10 profit per shirt, the math looks like this:

| Component | Amount |
|---|---|
| Supplier base cost | $9.50 |
| Shipping (passed to buyer or built in) | $4.99 |
| Etsy transaction fee (6.5% of $24.49) | ~$1.59 |
| Etsy listing fee | $0.20 |
| Your profit target | $10.00 |
| **List price** | **$24.99** |

(Source: Etsy Fee Schedule, as of 2026)

On Shopify, replace Etsy fees with your Shopify subscription cost spread across projected monthly sales, plus Shopify Payments processing fees — 2.9% + $0.30 per transaction as of 2026.

Don't race to the bottom on pricing. Check competitor prices to understand the market range, but competing on price alone is a losing strategy in POD where margins are already thin. A Shopify Plus analysis of successful merchants (2025) found that stores competing primarily on price have lower customer lifetime values and higher churn than those competing on design quality and brand identity.

Use psychological pricing — $24.99 converts better than $25.00. Consider bundles like "Buy 2 tees, save $5" to raise your average order value (AOV). Revisit your pricing every 90 days as supplier costs, USPS/UPS shipping rates, and platform fees change. ## Step 6: Write Product Listings That Rank and Convert

Your product title is the single most important ranking factor on both Etsy and Shopify search. Place your main keyword within the first 40 characters. Instead of "Cool Funny Shirt for Disc Golf Lovers," write "Disc Golf Shirt for Men – Funny Retirement Gift for Dad" so the primary keyword leads.

Write bullet points that answer three questions: Who is this for? What makes it special? What does it look and feel like? Include specific details — fabric weight (e.g., "5.3 oz ring-spun cotton"), available sizes, care instructions (machine wash cold, tumble dry low). Generic descriptions like "great quality shirt" tell buyers nothing and don't help with search rankings.

Add descriptive alt text to every product image for accessibility and SEO. Describe both the design and the product — for example, "Black unisex t-shirt with disc golf retirement design in white text." On Etsy, fill all 13 available tags with long-tail keyword variations. Don't waste tags on single words like "shirt." Use phrases like "disc golf retirement gift" or "funny disc golfer tee men."

Pair flat-lay mockups with at least one lifestyle photo showing the product in context. Listings with lifestyle imagery see up to 32% higher conversion rates on Etsy, according to Marmalead Seller Data (2025). If you ordered samples in Step 2 — which you should have — use your own product photography for at least one image slot.

## Step 7: Drive Traffic With a Simple Marketing Plan

Organic content on Pinterest and TikTok is the best starting point for POD sellers with small budgets. Both platforms are visual-first and reward consistent posting. Pin your product mockups and lifestyle images to niche-specific Pinterest boards. Pinterest works like a visual search engine, and pins can drive traffic for months after posting. On TikTok, show your design process — time-lapse videos of creating artwork in Adobe Illustrator or Canva consistently perform well.

Build an email list from day one using Klaviyo or Mailchimp. Offer a 10% discount code or a free digital download — like a phone wallpaper matching your designs — in exchange for a signup. Email subscribers convert at 3–5x the rate of social media followers (Source: Klaviyo Benchmark Report, 2026). A list of 100 subscribers typically outperforms 1,000 Instagram followers for direct sales.

Run Etsy Ads at $1–$3 per day for your first 30 days. The goal isn't immediate profit — it's data. Which listings get clicks? Which ones convert? After 30 days, cut the losers and increase spend on winners. Use Instagram Reels to show behind-the-scenes content: packing orders, reviewing samples, reacting to your first sale.

Only move to Meta Ads after you've proven organic demand and know your conversion rate. Running paid ads without conversion data is one of the fastest ways to burn through a launch budget.

**Real-world example:** A seller named Marcus launched a disc golf apparel brand in early 2026. He posted three TikToks per week showing his design process and hit 50 sales within 45 days — all from organic traffic before spending a dollar on ads.

## Step 8: Handle Orders, Customer Service, and Reviews

Once orders start coming in, your reputation depends on how you handle the post-purchase experience. Set up auto-reply messages in Etsy (via **Shop Manager → Messages → Auto-reply**) or your Shopify help desk for the three most common questions: "Where is my order?", "What size should I get?", and "Can I change or cancel my order?"

Monitor your POD supplier's production dashboard daily during your first two weeks of sales. Check that each order moves from "received" to "printed" to "shipped" within the expected timeframe. If an order stalls, contact your supplier immediately — don't wait for the customer to complain. Merchants who proactively message buyers about delays ("Your order is taking a bit longer than usual — here's your updated tracking") get far fewer negative reviews than those who stay silent.

Have a written script ready for refund and reprint requests. Something like: "I'm sorry this didn't meet your expectations. I'd like to offer a free reprint or a full refund — which would you prefer?" This resolves most issues and often saves the review.

On Etsy, you're allowed one follow-up message to ask satisfied buyers for a review. Keep it simple and genuine. Don't offer incentives for reviews — that violates Etsy's Terms of Service and can result in shop penalties. Log every complaint in a spreadsheet so you can spot patterns. If three customers say the sizing runs small on one product, update your listing with a sizing note immediately.

The Better Business Bureau recommends responding to customer issues within 48 hours for small businesses. On Etsy specifically, response time affects your "Star Seller" status, which requires a 95% response rate within 24 hours (Source: Etsy Star Seller Requirements, 2025).

## Print on Demand Launch Checklist: Quick-Reference Summary

Copy or print this checklist and revisit it every 90 days as your store grows:

- [ ] **Niche validated** — Confirmed demand via Google Trends, Etsy search, and Amazon Merch on Demand bestseller ranks
- [ ] **Supplier chosen** — Compared Printful, Printify, and Gelato; ordered samples from at least two
- [ ] **Artwork created** — 300 DPI minimum, proper bleed/safe zones, trademark search completed at USPTO.gov
- [ ] **Store set up** — Shopify or Etsy configured with bio, policies, payment processing, and sales tax collection
- [ ] **Pricing calculated** — Base cost + fees + margin formula applied; psychological pricing used
- [ ] **Listings optimized** — Keywords in titles, all Etsy tags filled, alt text on images, lifestyle photos included
- [ ] **Marketing launched** — Pinterest and TikTok posting, email list started, Etsy Ads running at $1–$3/day
- [ ] **Customer service ready** — Auto-replies set, refund/reprint script written, review follow-up message drafted
- [ ] **Analytics installed** — Google Analytics, Meta Pixel, and Google Merchant Center connected

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How long does it take to launch a print on demand store?
Most sellers can complete setup in 1–2 weeks if they follow a checklist. Niche research and design creation take the most time. Rushing this phase typically leads to poor sales, so plan for at least 7 days of research and design work before your first listing goes live.

### Do I need a business license to sell print on demand in the US?
Requirements vary by state. Many solo sellers start as sole proprietors without a formal LLC, but you should register for sales tax collection in states where you have nexus. Check your state's secretary of state website and consult a tax professional for guidance specific to your situation.

### Which print on demand supplier is best for US shipping speed?
Printful and Printify both have US-based print facilities that typically ship within 2–5 business days domestically. Gelato also has US nodes and is competitive for sellers who want eco-friendly options. Always order a sample to compare actual quality and delivery speed before selling to customers.

### Can I sell print on demand products on Etsy and Shopify at the same time?
Yes. Most POD suppliers like Printify and Printful integrate with both platforms. You can sync the same product to multiple channels and manage orders from one dashboard. Keep your inventory and pricing consistent across both storefronts to avoid customer confusion.

### What file format should I use for print on demand designs?
PNG with a transparent background at 300 DPI is the standard across most suppliers. Some suppliers also accept PDF or SVG for vector-based artwork, which is useful for designs that need to scale across multiple product sizes. Always check the specific template dimensions for each product type before uploading your file.

### How do I avoid selling designs that infringe on trademarks?
Search the USPTO trademark database at USPTO.gov before listing any phrase or image. Avoid fan art, sports team logos, character names, and celebrity likenesses unless you have an explicit license. When in doubt, create original artwork and consult an intellectual property attorney — a single consultation typically costs $150–$300 and can prevent a shop shutdown.

## Why You Need a Shopify Store Checklist

Launching a Shopify store without a structured checklist is like opening a physical shop with the cash register unplugged. Roughly 20–30% of new Shopify stores go live with broken checkout flows. That costs merchants thousands in lost revenue during the first week alone (Source: Shopify Community Data, 2025). Abandoned carts, missing legal pages, and wrong shipping rates destroy customer trust before you've earned any.

This checklist covers six phases — from initial setup through your first 30 days of sales. Follow it in order and you'll avoid the most expensive beginner mistakes. Skip around and you'll spend twice as long fixing problems after launch.

## Phase 1: Store Setup Checklist — Build a Solid Foundation Before Touching Product Pages

Your store's foundation determines everything that follows. Lock in these basics before you touch product pages or marketing.

- **Choose and customize a Shopify theme.** Start with a free option like Dawn or Craft, or invest in a paid theme from the Shopify Theme Store ($180–$400 as of 2026). Match your theme to your catalog size — Dawn works well for smaller catalogs (under 100 SKUs), while paid themes like Prestige handle larger inventories with advanced filtering and mega menus.
- **Add your store name, logo, and brand colors.** Apply these consistently across your header, favicon, email templates, and checkout. Mismatched colors between storefront and checkout can increase bounce rates by up to 38% (Source: Baymard Institute, 2025).
- **Set up a custom domain.** Buy through Shopify (Settings > Domains) or connect an existing domain via Shopify's DNS settings. A `.com` domain still converts better than alternatives for US audiences, according to Shopify's own conversion benchmarks.
- **Configure store defaults.** Set your currency to USD, timezone to your local zone, and weight units to pounds or ounces. Find these under Settings > General.
- **Add legal pages.** Use Shopify's built-in templates (Settings > Policies) for your refund policy, privacy policy, and terms of service as a starting point. Then customize them to reflect your actual business. Generic templates won't protect you in a dispute and raise red flags with careful shoppers.
- **Build your navigation.** Create a main menu (shop, collections, about, contact), a footer menu (legal pages, FAQ, shipping info), and enable breadcrumbs in your theme settings. Breadcrumbs show users their path through the site — for example, Home > Men > Shirts. They also help search engines read your site structure.

**Example:** The DTC skincare brand *Dew Mighty* launched on Shopify with a customized Dawn theme, a clean three-item main menu, and a memorable `.com` domain. Their setup helped them hit a 4.2% conversion rate in their first month — well above the 1–3% Shopify average (Source: Shopify Plus Case Studies, 2025).

## Phase 2: Product Pages Checklist — Every Element Should Build Buyer Confidence

Your product pages do the selling. Every element — from the title to the last image — either builds confidence or creates doubt. Merchants who rush this phase often find themselves reworking pages within weeks after seeing poor conversion data.

- **Write unique product titles with primary keywords.** Instead of "Blue Shirt," use "Men's Organic Cotton Oxford Shirt – Navy Blue." This helps both shoppers and search engines understand what you're selling.
- **Upload high-quality images.** Use a minimum of three images per product at 2048×2048px resolution. Show the product from multiple angles, in context (lifestyle shot), and with a close-up of materials or details. Shopify automatically compresses and serves WebP format to supported browsers.
- **Add alt text to every product image.** Alt text is the descriptive text attached to an image for screen readers and search engines. Be specific: "Navy blue men's oxford shirt on model, front view" beats "IMG_4392."
- **Write benefit-led product descriptions.** Skip manufacturer copy. Tell the customer how the product solves their problem. Use bullet points for easy scanning, and put the most important benefit first.
- **Set up variants, SKUs, and inventory tracking.** Configure sizes, colors, and materials as proper variants in each product's admin page. Turn on inventory tracking to prevent overselling — Shopify flags out-of-stock items automatically when this is active.
- **Install a product reviews app before launch.** Apps like Judge.me (free tier available as of 2026) or Loox ($9.99/month) display customer ratings and testimonials on your product page. Products with reviews convert 270% better than those without (Source: Spiegel Research Center, 2021, reconfirmed in their 2025 update).
- **Enable product schema markup.** Schema markup is structured data that helps search engines show rich results — star ratings, pricing, availability — directly in listings. Most modern Shopify themes include this by default, but verify it using Google's Rich Results Test tool.

**Limitation to keep in mind:** Shopify's native product page editor has real constraints. You can't easily add comparison tables, ingredient breakdowns, or tabbed content without a page builder app like Shogun or GemPages, or custom Liquid code.

## Phase 3: Payments and Checkout Checklist — A Broken Checkout Costs You More Than Any Other Error

A broken or confusing checkout kills conversions faster than anything else on your site. This phase is non-negotiable.

- **Enable Shopify Payments.** It's built in, eliminates the extra 0.5–2% third-party transaction fee (the percentage varies by plan tier), and supports major credit cards. If Shopify Payments isn't available in your region, connect Stripe or PayPal. See our [Shopify Payments setup guide](/how-to-set-up-shopify-payments) for step-by-step instructions.
- **Activate Shop Pay, Apple Pay, and Google Pay.** These one-tap checkout options reduce friction significantly. Shop Pay alone increases checkout-to-conversion rates by up to 50% compared to guest checkout (Source: Shopify, 2026).
- **Test checkout with a real transaction.** Place an actual order with your own credit card, then refund it. This confirms your payment gateway, shipping rates, tax calculations, and order confirmation emails all work. Merchants who skip this step find out something is broken only after losing real customers.
- **Set up shipping zones and rates.** Define domestic zones (contiguous US, Alaska, Hawaii) and set flat rates, weight-based rates, or calculated carrier rates from USPS, UPS, or FedEx. Consider a free shipping threshold — orders over $50 with free shipping typically see around 30% higher average order values (Source: National Retail Federation, 2025).
- **Configure tax settings.** Shopify auto-calculates US sales tax by state (Settings > Taxes and duties), but verify your nexus obligations for 2026. Nexus is the legal threshold — based on physical presence or economic activity — that requires you to collect sales tax in a given state. Rules vary by state, so talk to a tax professional if you sell across multiple states.
- **Enable abandoned cart recovery.** Shopify includes basic recovery emails on all plans (Settings > Checkout > Abandoned checkouts). For more advanced flows with custom timing and segmentation, connect Klaviyo (free up to 500 contacts as of 2026). Our [abandoned cart recovery guide](/shopify-abandoned-cart-recovery) covers both options.
- **Customize Shopify Checkout branding.** Add your logo, match your brand colors, and include trust badges — SSL lock icon, accepted payment logos, money-back guarantee. Find these under Settings > Checkout > Checkout customization. A branded checkout reduces cart abandonment by keeping the visual experience consistent.

**Example:** *Ridge Wallet* credits a big part of their conversion rate improvement to enabling Shop Pay and Apple Pay. Their one-tap checkout reduced average checkout time from 90 seconds to under 15 seconds (Source: Shopify Commerce Trends, 2025).

## Phase 4: SEO Checklist — Get Google Indexing Your Store Correctly From Day One

Search engine optimization is typically your most cost-effective long-term traffic source. Handle these items before launch so Google indexes your store correctly from the start.

- **Edit title tags and meta descriptions.** Write unique tags for your homepage, every collection page, and your top 20 products. Keep titles under 60 characters and meta descriptions under 155 characters. Include your primary keyword naturally — no stuffing.
- **Submit your XML sitemap to Google Search Console.** Your sitemap lives at `yourstore.com/sitemap.xml`. Submit it immediately after removing your store password so Google can start crawling. Shopify generates this sitemap automatically and updates it when you add or remove pages.
- **Fix duplicate content issues.** Shopify creates duplicate URLs for products listed in multiple collections — for example, `/collections/shirts/products/navy-oxford` and `/products/navy-oxford`. Verify that canonical tags point to the primary product URL. Most Shopify themes handle this correctly, but check using "View Page Source" in your browser.
- **Enable Shopify's built-in blog.** Publish at least 3–5 keyword-targeted posts before launch. Blog content drives organic traffic and helps you rank for informational queries. A coffee equipment store, for example, might publish "How to Dial In Your Espresso Grind Size" to reach buyers who are still researching.
- **Compress your images.** Use TinyPNG, Shopify's built-in image optimizer, or an app like Crush.pics. Large uncompressed images are the most common cause of slow Shopify stores.
- **Check your page speed score.** Run your homepage and a product page through Google PageSpeed Insights. Target 80+ on mobile. Scores below 50 correlate with significantly higher bounce rates (Source: Google Web Performance Report, 2025). Shopify's infrastructure handles server response times well, but third-party apps and unoptimized images drag scores down fast.
- **Add structured data for products, reviews, and breadcrumbs.** This gets you rich results in Google — star ratings, pricing, and availability shown directly in search listings, which typically improves click-through rates.

**Tradeoff worth noting:** Shopify's URL structure isn't fully customizable. Collection pages always use `/collections/`, product pages always use `/products/`, and you can't remove these prefixes without a headless setup. This is a minor SEO limitation, but it rarely causes meaningful ranking problems for most stores.

## Phase 5: Marketing and Analytics Checklist — Measure From Day One So You Can Optimize From Day Two

You can't improve what you don't measure. Set up tracking and marketing channels before you launch. Retrofitting analytics after launch means losing your most valuable early data.

- **Install Google Analytics 4.** Connect GA4 through Shopify's Google channel (Sales channels > Google) or use Google Tag Manager for more control. Verify that purchase events, add-to-cart events, and page views are all firing correctly using GA4's DebugView. Our [GA4 Shopify setup guide](/google-analytics-4-shopify-setup) walks through the full process.
- **Set up Meta Pixel.** Install via Meta Business Suite and verify key events: ViewContent, AddToCart, InitiateCheckout, and Purchase. Without verified events, your Meta ad campaigns can't optimize for conversions — you'd be paying for impressions with no learning signal.
- **Connect Google Search Console.** Verify domain ownership using the DNS method — it's the most reliable. This free tool shows which queries drive impressions and clicks to your store, and flags crawl or indexing issues.
- **Create a welcome email flow.** Use Klaviyo or Shopify Email to send a 3-email welcome sequence to new subscribers. A welcome flow is an automated email series triggered when someone joins your list. Welcome flows generate 3× more revenue per recipient than standard promotional emails (Source: Klaviyo Benchmarks Report, 2026).
- **Set up Google Merchant Center.** Connect your product feed for Google Shopping ads. Shopify's Google channel syncs your inventory automatically, keeping prices and availability accurate. Make sure your product data — titles, GTINs, images — meets Google's feed requirements to avoid disapprovals.
- **Add social proof elements.** Display trust badges in your footer, customer counts ("Join 5,000+ happy customers" — only if accurate), and any press mentions on your homepage.
- **Schedule launch-day social media posts.** Prepare content for Instagram, TikTok, and Pinterest at minimum. Short-form product videos outperform static images by 2–3× on engagement metrics (Source: Meta Business Insights, 2025).

**Example:** When *Graza* — the squeezable olive oil brand — launched their Shopify store, they had GA4, Meta Pixel, and a Klaviyo welcome flow running from day one. Their email list generated 35% of first-month revenue (Source: Klaviyo Customer Stories, 2025).

## Phase 6: Pre-Launch Final Checks — Skip Nothing on Your Last Pass

This is your last pass before the world sees your store. Treat it like a pilot's pre-flight checklist — skip nothing.

- **Remove password protection.** Go to Online Store > Preferences and disable the password page. Your store is now live.
- **Test every link.** Click through your main menu, footer menu, product pages, collection pages, and any in-page links. Use a free broken link checker like Screaming Frog (up to 500 URLs free as of 2026) to catch 404 errors.
- **Check mobile responsiveness.** Open your store on both an iPhone and an Android device. Tap every button, scroll through product galleries, and complete a test checkout on mobile. Over 72% of Shopify traffic comes from mobile devices (Source: Shopify, 2026). A desktop-only QA pass is not enough.
- **Review all notification emails.** Go to Settings > Notifications and preview every template: order confirmation, shipping confirmation, refund notification, and account welcome. Add your logo and adjust the tone to match your brand.
- **Test your contact form and live chat.** Submit a test inquiry through your contact form and confirm it arrives in your inbox. If you've installed a live chat app, verify it loads and works on both desktop and mobile.
- **Review Shopify Markets settings.** If you plan to sell internationally, configure Shopify Markets (Settings > Markets) with the correct currencies, languages, and duties/taxes for each region. If you're US-only at launch, disable international markets to avoid confusing pricing for visitors from other countries.

**Example:** *Olipop* ran a full pre-launch QA sprint using a 47-item internal checklist before launching their DTC Shopify store. Their team caught a broken collection filter on mobile that was hiding 40% of their products from phone users — a revenue-killing bug that was invisible on desktop.

## Post-Launch Shopify Checklist: Your First 30 Days Determine Momentum

Launching is just the starting line. Your first 30 days determine whether you build momentum or stall. Merchants who treat launch day as the finish line usually see conversion rates plateau or drop within weeks.

- **Monitor GA4 daily.** Watch traffic sources, bounce rate, and session duration. Find which channels bring the most engaged visitors, then put more budget there.
- **Check Google Search Console weekly.** Look for crawl errors, indexing issues, and new keyword impressions. Fix any "Page with redirect" or "Not found (404)" errors right away — they signal to Google that your site has quality problems.
- **Review Shopify's analytics dashboard.** Compare your conversion rate against the industry average of 1–3% (Source: Shopify Benchmarks, 2026). If you're below 1%, fix product pages and checkout friction before spending more on ads. Sending traffic to a broken funnel wastes money.
- **A/B test your homepage.** Swap your hero image, headline, and CTA button copy. Even a small change — "Shop Now" vs. "See What's New" — can move conversion rates by 10–20%. Use a Shopify A/B testing app to run controlled experiments rather than guessing.
- **Collect customer feedback.** Send a post-purchase survey via Typeform or Google Forms. Ask: "What almost stopped you from buying?" The answers reveal friction points your analytics can't show — unclear sizing, shipping cost surprises, or trust concerns.
- **Install upsell and cross-sell apps.** Apps like ReConvert (for post-purchase upsells) and Frequently Bought Together increase average order value (AOV) — the average amount a customer spends per transaction. Visit our list of [essential Shopify apps for new stores](/shopify-apps-for-new-stores).
- **Send your first email campaign.** Email your subscriber list with a launch announcement, a founder story, or a limited-time offer. Don't wait. Email subscribers are your warmest audience and typically convert at 3–5× the rate of cold traffic.

## FAQ

### How long does it take to launch a Shopify store?

Most stores can launch in 3–14 days if you follow a structured checklist. A basic store with under 50 products can go live in a weekend. Custom builds with apps, integrations, and large product catalogs typically take 1–2 weeks.

### What should I do before removing the Shopify password page?

Complete all checklist phases: test checkout with a real transaction, confirm all product images and descriptions are in place, verify legal pages are published, and check mobile responsiveness on at least two devices.

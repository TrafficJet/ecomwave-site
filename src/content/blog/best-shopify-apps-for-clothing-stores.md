Running a clothing store on Shopify means dealing with problems other niches simply don't face. The right apps cut return costs, raise average order value, and turn one-time browsers into repeat buyers. This guide covers the best Shopify apps for clothing stores across every category that matters to your bottom line.

## Why Clothing Stores Need Specialized Shopify Apps

Apparel brands deal with sizing confusion, high return rates, seasonal inventory swings, and constant pressure to present products well visually. A generic Shopify setup won't fix these problems. That means you're losing revenue every day.

The average return rate for online clothing sits between 20% and 30% (National Retail Federation, 2025 Consumer Returns Report). The right mix of apps — size guides, exchange-first return portals — can pull that number down and protect your margins.

Apps in this guide were selected on four criteria: free trial availability, transparent pricing tiers, average review scores of 4.5 stars or higher on the Shopify App Store, and popularity among US merchants as of 2026. If you're just [getting started with your clothing store on Shopify](/how-to-start-a-clothing-store-on-shopify), these tools give you a strong foundation from day one.

## Best Shopify Apps for Product Reviews and Social Proof

Online shoppers want to see how clothes look on real people — not models. Photo and video reviews are the closest thing to a fitting room. Four apps stand out here: **Judge.me**, **Loox**, **Yotpo**, and **Okendo**.

**Judge.me** has a free tier with unlimited review requests, photo reviews, and a review carousel widget. **Loox** starts at $9.99/month (as of 2026) and focuses on visual reviews, with automatic photo request emails. **Yotpo** and **Okendo** offer more advanced UGC (user-generated content — photos, videos, and reviews created by customers) tools, including direct feeds from Instagram and TikTok Shop. Okendo also collects attribute-based reviews covering fit, quality, and comfort, which helps future buyers make sizing decisions.

**Fera.ai** is worth mentioning too. It pulls reviews from multiple platforms — Amazon, Etsy, Shopify — into one display widget. Useful if you sell across channels.

**Real-world example:** Brooklyn-based boutique Adorn Collective added Loox photo reviews to their product pages in early 2026. Within 45 days, conversion rate went up 18%. Customers said seeing "real body types" in reviews gave them the confidence to buy. For apparel, customer photos often outperform professional photography as a selling tool.

## Best Shopify Apps for Email and SMS Marketing

**Klaviyo** and **Attentive** dominate for apparel brands on Shopify. They work well together.

**Klaviyo** handles email with a native Shopify integration that pulls in order data, browsing behavior, and product catalog info automatically. In 2026, Klaviyo added AI send-time optimization — it studies each subscriber's engagement history and sends emails when that person is most likely to open. You can build abandoned cart flows, back-in-stock alerts, and post-purchase sequences that suggest matching items.

One tactic that works well for clothing stores: size-specific segmentation. Email only the customers who buy XL when a new XL-heavy drop lands. Don't blast your whole list. Merchants who try this find that targeted size-based emails get two to three times the click-through rate of general promotional sends.

**Attentive** is the go-to for SMS. SMS open rates hit roughly 98%, compared to 20–22% for email (Attentive, 2025 SMS Marketing Benchmarks Report). That makes it the right channel for flash sales, limited drops, and restock alerts where urgency drives action. For building these flows in more detail, see our [Shopify email marketing guide](/shopify-email-marketing-guide).

**Limitation to consider:** Klaviyo's free tier covers only 250 contacts and 500 email sends per month. Most clothing brands outgrow this fast. Budget $20–$45/month for the next tier. Attentive uses custom pricing and doesn't publish rates publicly — request a quote during your trial.

**Setup walkthrough:** To build a Klaviyo abandoned cart flow, go to **Klaviyo dashboard > Flows > Create Flow > select the "Abandoned Cart" template**. Add a conditional split based on cart value — for example, send a 10% discount only when the cart exceeds $100. Then insert dynamic product blocks that pull in the exact items left behind, including size and color.

## Best Shopify Apps for Upselling and Cross-Selling

If your average order value (AOV — the average dollar amount per transaction) isn't moving, **Rebuy**, **Bold Upsell**, and **LimeSpot** are worth testing.

**Rebuy** is the standout for fashion stores. Its Smart Cart feature shows AI-powered "complete the look" recommendations directly inside the slide-out cart — a belt with jeans, a scarf with a coat. The widget works with Shopify's native checkout as of 2026, so no Checkout Extensibility workarounds are needed. Apparel merchants using AI-driven recommendations report AOV lifts of 10–15% on average (Rebuy, 2026 Merchant Benchmark Data).

**Bold Upsell** handles straightforward post-purchase upsells well — "Add matching socks for $8?" — while **LimeSpot** focuses on personalized collection page recommendations based on browsing history.

**Tradeoff to note:** Rebuy starts at $99/month (as of 2026). It makes more sense for stores doing at least $10,000/month in revenue. For smaller stores, Bold Upsell at $9.99/month gives a better cost-to-value ratio. Merchants running fewer than 500 orders per month often get comparable percentage lifts from simpler tools without the higher fixed cost.

## Best Shopify Apps for Returns and Exchanges

Returns are the single biggest margin killer for clothing stores. Wrong size, unexpected color, and poor fit cause most apparel returns. **Loop Returns** and **ReturnGO** both turn this cost center into a retention tool.

**Loop Returns** shows shoppers alternative sizes or colors before offering a refund. Its bonus credit feature gives customers extra store credit — "$5 bonus if you exchange instead of refund" — which keeps money inside your store. Loop's Starter plan is $29/month (as of 2026), which works for smaller boutiques. Mid-size brands processing 200+ returns per month usually move to the Essential plan at $115/month for the analytics.

**ReturnGO** starts at $9.95/month. It offers a self-service return portal with automated label generation. A solid budget option if you handle fewer than 100 returns monthly.

**Limitation:** Loop requires the customer to log in or enter their order number and zip code to start a return. That's a small friction step. But Loop reports fewer than 3% of customers abandon the flow at that point.

**Case study:** Portland-based indie brand Fern & Thread installed Loop Returns in January 2026. In Q1, their refund rate dropped 22%. Exchange requests jumped from 15% to 41% of all return cases, keeping an estimated $18,000 in revenue that would have otherwise left. The founder said the $5 bonus credit was the single most effective lever — customers treated it as a reward, not a sales push. Read more about [reducing your ecommerce return rates](/how-to-reduce-ecommerce-return-rates).

## Best Shopify Apps for Inventory Management

Clothing inventory is uniquely complex. You're tracking products, size variants, colorways, and seasonal collections simultaneously. **Inventory Planner** and Shopify's native **Stocky** app both handle this well.

**Inventory Planner** forecasts demand at the individual SKU (stock-keeping unit — a unique identifier for each product variant) and size level. If size M sells twice as fast as size S, the app flags it and generates reorder recommendations automatically. This matters most for limited-edition drops, where overordering kills margins. Brands using Inventory Planner report overstock reductions of up to 25% (Inventory Planner, 2026 Case Studies).

**Stocky** comes free with Shopify POS Pro. It handles basic purchase orders, inventory transfers, and multi-location tracking. If you run a warehouse plus retail pop-ups, Stocky keeps counts in sync across locations.

**Tradeoff:** Inventory Planner starts at $249.99/month (as of 2026). That's a real investment for smaller brands. If you carry fewer than 200 SKUs, Shopify's native inventory reports combined with Stocky may be enough. Inventory Planner typically pays for itself once a brand reaches 500+ SKUs across multiple size runs and seasonal collections. For more on this topic, visit our [Shopify inventory management tips](/shopify-inventory-management-tips).

## Best Shopify Apps for Visual Merchandising and Page Building

How your store looks directly affects how long shoppers stay and whether they buy. **Shogun** and **PageFly** are the top page builders for creating lookbook layouts, editorial collection pages, and campaign landing pages — no code required.

Both use drag-and-drop editors. But **Shogun** stands out with built-in A/B testing. You can test two collection page layouts — grid versus editorial, for example — and see which one converts better. **PageFly** starts with a limited free plan and is a good fit for boutiques that need strong pages on a tighter budget. Shogun's paid plans begin at $39/month (as of 2026).

Mobile-first design is not optional. Over 70% of fashion e-commerce traffic comes from mobile devices (Statista, Global Mobile Commerce Report, 2025). Both Shogun and PageFly let you design mobile layouts independently from desktop, so lookbook pages load fast and display correctly on any screen.

**Performance consideration:** Page builder apps add JavaScript and CSS that can slow your pages down. According to the Baymard Institute (2024), 53% of mobile users leave a page that takes more than three seconds to load. After installing either app, run a test in Google PageSpeed Insights and remove unused sections or widgets. If you're also evaluating your store's overall design, see our list of the [best Shopify themes for clothing stores](/best-shopify-themes-for-clothing-stores).

## Best Shopify Apps for Customer Support

**Gorgias** is the leading helpdesk app for Shopify clothing stores. It puts email, live chat, Instagram DMs, Facebook messages, and TikTok comments into one inbox. One agent can handle a sizing question from Instagram and an order status request from email without switching tabs.

Gorgias's AI auto-responses handle repetitive questions — "What size should I order?" and "Where's my package?" — without a human. As of 2026, the AI resolves up to 40% of tickets on its own (Gorgias, 2026 Platform Data). The app also connects directly with Loop Returns and Klaviyo, so support agents see return status and email history right in context.

Gorgias starts at $10/month for up to 50 tickets (as of 2026). Brands handling more than 300 tickets per month should plan for the $60/month tier, which includes automation and extra integrations.

**"We cut our average response time from 14 hours to under 3 hours after switching to Gorgias. For a clothing brand where customers want sizing help before they buy, speed directly impacts conversion."** — Sarah M., founder of a DTC womenswear brand on Shopify

For smaller boutiques watching costs, **Tidio** offers live chat with basic automation on a free plan. The trade-off is that Tidio has no deep Shopify order data integration, so agents have to look up orders manually in the Shopify admin.

## How to Choose the Right Apps for Your Clothing Store

Start with three to five apps that fix your biggest revenue gaps. Installing too many at once slows your store's load time, which hurts both user experience and SEO. Every app script adds weight to your pages. Nielsen Norman Group research (2023) found that even a one-second page load delay can reduce conversions by up to 7%.

Before installing anything, look at your conversion funnel. Where are shoppers dropping off? If it's the product page, invest in reviews and better visuals first. If it's post-purchase, focus on returns and email flows. Also check that any app is compatible with your current [Shopify theme](/best-shopify-themes-for-clothing-stores) — read the documentation or ask their support team directly.

Use Shopify's built-in analytics at **Shopify Admin > Analytics > Reports** to measure each app's ROI after 30 days. If an upsell app isn't moving your AOV, remove it. Nearly every app on this list has a free trial. Test before you pay.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the best Shopify app for managing clothing returns?

Loop Returns is the top pick for clothing stores in 2026. It pushes exchanges over refunds using bonus credit, which keeps revenue in your store. ReturnGO is a solid budget alternative starting at $9.95/month.

### Do I need a paid Shopify plan to use these apps?

Most apps on this list work with any Shopify plan, including Basic Shopify at $39/month (as of 2026). Some advanced features — Klaviyo's full segmentation tools or Gorgias's automation rules, for example — require a paid app tier regardless of your Shopify plan.

### How many apps should a new clothing store install?

Start with three to five apps targeting your biggest gaps. Reviews, email marketing, and returns are usually the highest-impact categories. Too many apps slow your store and hurt SEO. Add more only after you've measured results over at least 30 days.

### Are there free Shopify apps that work well for fashion brands?

Yes. Judge.me has a solid free tier for photo and video reviews. Shopify's native Stocky app handles basic inventory management at no cost with POS Pro. Klaviyo, PageFly, Fera.ai, and Tidio also offer free plans with usage caps or limited features.

### Which Shopify app is best for increasing average order value in clothing stores?

Rebuy is widely used by apparel brands for its AI-powered "complete the look" recommendations and smart cart upsells. Merchants report AOV lifts of 10–15% within the first 60 days (Rebuy, 2026 Merchant Benchmark Data). For stores under $10,000/month in revenue, Bold Upsell at $9.99/month is the lower-cost entry point.

### Can Shopify apps help with size recommendations to reduce returns?

Yes. Size recommendation apps like Kiwi Sizing and Fit Quiz plug directly into Shopify product pages. When customers find their correct size before buying, return rates drop. Some brands report a 30%+ reduction in size-related returns. These tools work by asking shoppers a few questions about their measurements or comparing fit to brands they already own.

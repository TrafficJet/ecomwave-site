## Why You Need an Ecommerce Checklist in 2026

US ecommerce sales are projected to surpass $1.3 trillion in 2026 (Source: eMarketer, 2026). That number sounds good until you see how many stores launch broken — missing legal pages, slow load times, checkout flows that push buyers away.

The average cart abandonment rate sits around 70%. A big part of that comes from preventable problems: surprise fees, forced account creation, missing payment options (Source: Baymard Institute, 2025). Merchants who skip a structured launch process spend their first three months fixing things a checklist would have caught in an afternoon.

An ecommerce checklist is not a one-time task. It is a repeatable system covering both your initial launch and ongoing growth — from platform setup to post-launch A/B testing. Skipping steps costs you sales. It can also mean FTC penalties, ADA lawsuits, or search rankings that take months to recover.

This guide walks through every critical task across nine sections. Bookmark it, share it with your team, and revisit it every quarter.

## Platform & Tech Stack Setup: Build a Fast, Secure Foundation

Your platform choice shapes everything that follows. **Shopify** (plans start at $39/month as of 2025) is the fastest path to launch — built-in hosting, PCI compliance included. **WooCommerce** is free as a plugin but needs separate hosting ($15–$50+/month), security plugins, and ongoing maintenance. **BigCommerce** (plans start at $39/month as of 2025) sits between the two, with strong multi-channel features out of the box.

The tradeoff is real. Shopify limits code-level customization unless you're on Shopify Plus ($2,300+/month as of 2025). WooCommerce gives full control but puts technical responsibility on you. For a deeper look, see our [Shopify vs. WooCommerce guide](/shopify-vs-woocommerce) or our [best ecommerce platforms](/best-ecommerce-platforms) roundup.

Here's your tech stack checklist:

- [ ] Choose and configure your platform (Shopify, WooCommerce, or BigCommerce)
- [ ] Register a custom domain and confirm your SSL certificate is active (look for the padlock icon in your browser's address bar)
- [ ] Connect a CDN — a content delivery network that serves your pages from servers closer to each visitor — such as Cloudflare or your platform's built-in CDN for fast load times across all US regions
- [ ] Set up a staging environment so you never push untested changes to your live store (on Shopify, navigate to **Online Store > Themes > Actions > Duplicate** to create a test copy)
- [ ] Verify your Core Web Vitals pass Google thresholds — LCP (Largest Contentful Paint, measuring how fast your main content loads) under 2.5s, CLS (Cumulative Layout Shift, measuring visual stability) under 0.1, INP (Interaction to Next Paint, measuring responsiveness) under 200ms (Source: Google Web Vitals documentation, 2025)
- [ ] Install Google Analytics 4 and Google Tag Manager
- [ ] Enable two-factor authentication on every admin account

**Example:** Allbirds runs on Shopify Plus and scores green on all three Core Web Vitals metrics. That contributes directly to strong organic visibility and fast mobile conversions. Merchants who run PageSpeed Insights before launch regularly find render-blocking scripts or uncompressed images slowing their site by 1–3 seconds.

## Product Page & Catalog: Turn Browsers Into Buyers

Product pages are where money is made or lost. Every detail — image quality, schema markup (structured code that helps search engines understand your product data) — affects whether a visitor clicks "Add to Cart" or leaves. See our [product page optimization guide](/product-page-optimization) for more.

- [ ] Write unique product titles and descriptions (never copy manufacturer text — duplicate content hurts search rankings and gives shoppers no reason to choose you over a competitor)
- [ ] Upload high-resolution images (minimum 1000×1000px) with descriptive alt text for accessibility and SEO
- [ ] Add product videos or 360-degree views where possible — pages with video see up to 80% higher conversion (Source: Wyzowl State of Video Marketing report, 2025)
- [ ] Set correct SKUs, variants (size, color, material), and real-time inventory levels
- [ ] Add structured data (Product, Review, and Offer schema) so Google can display rich results like star ratings and pricing directly in search listings
- [ ] Display trust signals near the Add to Cart button: star ratings, review counts, and a return policy summary
- [ ] Ensure sold-out products show clear "Out of Stock" messaging with links to similar alternatives and an optional back-in-stock email notification

One thing to watch: product video adds page weight fast. Merchants who embed video directly instead of lazy-loading through YouTube or Vimeo often see LCP scores drop. Test your Core Web Vitals after adding any media.

**Example:** Ridge Wallet uses clean lifestyle photos, short benefit-focused descriptions, and visible review counts right below the product title. Their pages rank on page one for competitive terms like "slim wallet for men." It works because they combine social proof with fast-loading, benefit-focused content — a pattern repeated across high-performing DTC brands.

## SEO: Capture the 33% of Traffic That Starts With a Search

Organic search drives roughly 33% of all ecommerce traffic (Source: Semrush Ecommerce Study, 2026). Without optimization, that traffic goes to competitors. Our [ecommerce SEO guide](/ecommerce-seo-guide) covers advanced tactics. Start here:

- [ ] Research primary and long-tail keywords for every category and product page using tools like Semrush, Ahrefs, or Google Keyword Planner
- [ ] Write unique H1 tags and meta descriptions under 155 characters for each page
- [ ] Generate an XML sitemap and submit it to Google Search Console (on Shopify, your sitemap auto-generates at `yourstore.com/sitemap.xml`)
- [ ] Set up canonical tags — HTML elements that tell search engines which version of a page is the "original" — to prevent duplicate content from filters, sorting options, and pagination
- [ ] Build internal links between related products and categories (e.g., link running shoes to running socks)
- [ ] Optimize URLs: keep them short, hyphenated, and keyword-rich (`/womens-running-shoes` not `/product?id=4829`)
- [ ] Launch an SEO blog or buying guide section to capture top-of-funnel informational searches
- [ ] Submit your product feed to Google Merchant Center to appear in free Shopping listings

SEO results take three to six months for new stores. Plan paid advertising to cover traffic in the meantime. Merchants who expect organic results within weeks often quit their SEO efforts too early.

**Example:** Beardbrand built a content hub of grooming guides that ranks for hundreds of informational keywords like "how to trim a beard" and "best beard oils." Thousands of monthly visitors flow into their product catalog without paid ads. It took roughly 12–18 months of consistent publishing to reach that point.

## Payments & Checkout: Remove Every Reason Not to Buy

A clunky checkout kills conversions fast. Nearly 22% of shoppers abandon carts because the process is too complicated (Source: Baymard Institute Checkout Usability Study, 2025). Keep it simple and flexible.

- [ ] Offer at least three payment methods: credit/debit via Stripe or Shopify Payments, PayPal, and an accelerated option like Shop Pay or Apple Pay
- [ ] Enable guest checkout — do *not* force account creation before purchase (you can offer optional account creation on the thank-you page instead)
- [ ] Display accepted payment icons on product pages, the cart page, and the footer
- [ ] Test the full checkout flow on desktop, mobile, and tablet before launch — actually complete a real order and confirm the confirmation email arrives
- [ ] Set up address validation (via EasyPost or Shopify's built-in tool under **Settings > Shipping and delivery**) to reduce failed deliveries
- [ ] Confirm your PCI-DSS compliance status with your payment processor (Shopify handles this natively; WooCommerce requires a separate PCI compliance audit or a payment gateway like Stripe that handles card data off-site)
- [ ] Add a buy-now-pay-later (BNPL) option like Affirm, Klarna, or Afterpay to boost average order value (AOV — the average dollar amount per transaction)

A tradeoff with BNPL: it typically raises AOV, but these services charge merchants 2–8% per transaction on top of standard processing fees. For low-margin products, run the numbers before enabling it.

**Example:** Fashion retailer Princess Polly reported a 15% increase in AOV after adding Afterpay at checkout, with the biggest lift among 18–34-year-old shoppers (Source: Afterpay merchant case studies, 2024).

## Shipping & Fulfillment: Meet the Two-Day Expectation (or Set Clear Alternatives)

Shipping expectations in 2026 are aggressive. Over 60% of US shoppers expect free shipping above a certain threshold. 40% expect delivery within two days (Source: Shippo State of Shipping report, 2026). Your [ecommerce shipping strategy](/ecommerce-shipping-strategy) needs to match those expectations — or clearly explain why it doesn't.

- [ ] Define shipping zones that cover all 50 US states (don't forget Hawaii and Alaska — many stores accidentally exclude them, and merchants who sell outdoor or lifestyle products often find these are high-value customers)
- [ ] Integrate with at least one major carrier: UPS, FedEx, or USPS (most stores benefit from using two to compare rates by package size and destination)
- [ ] Set a free shipping threshold (e.g., "Free shipping on orders over $75") to incentivize larger carts — the threshold should sit 15–20% above your current AOV
- [ ] Configure automated tracking emails and SMS notifications at each fulfillment stage
- [ ] Display estimated delivery windows on product and cart pages — *before* the customer reaches checkout
- [ ] Create a self-service returns portal with label generation (apps like Loop Returns or Returnly integrate with Shopify via the App Store)
- [ ] Decide if same-day or two-day shipping is viable for metro areas and communicate eligibility clearly

Free shipping is not free for you. The cost gets absorbed into product pricing, offset by higher AOV at the threshold, or funded by margin. Merchants who set the free shipping threshold too low often erode profitability without noticing until they review unit economics months later.

**Example:** Chewy shows delivery date estimates on every product page ("Get it by Thursday, March 12") and offers free shipping over $35. That transparency cuts cart abandonment and sets clear expectations — Baymard Institute's research consistently ranks this as a top-five checkout optimization.

## Legal & Compliance: Avoid Fines, Lawsuits, and Store Shutdowns

Getting this wrong is expensive. ADA-related website accessibility lawsuits exceeded 4,000 filings in 2025 (Source: UsableNet Year-End Report, 2025). The FTC has also increased enforcement against deceptive online advertising. Legal compliance is not optional. Our [ecommerce legal requirements guide](/ecommerce-legal-requirements) covers each item in detail.

- [ ] Publish a Privacy Policy compliant with CCPA (California Consumer Privacy Act) and other state privacy laws — Colorado, Connecticut, Virginia, Texas, Oregon, and Montana all have active data privacy statutes as of 2025
- [ ] Add Terms & Conditions and a dedicated Return/Refund Policy page
- [ ] Display a cookie consent banner compliant with applicable US state laws (tools like OneTrust or Termly automate multi-state compliance)
- [ ] Verify all product descriptions meet FTC advertising guidelines — no unsubstantiated health claims, inflated "compare at" prices, or fabricated reviews
- [ ] Check your sales tax nexus obligations in every state where you have economic nexus, a concept established by the 2018 *South Dakota v. Wayfair* Supreme Court ruling that requires online sellers to collect sales tax once they exceed a state's revenue or transaction threshold (typically $100,000 in sales or 200 transactions)
- [ ] Audit your site against WCAG 2.2 AA standards to reduce ADA lawsuit risk (use tools like axe DevTools or WAVE for automated scanning, then follow up with manual keyboard and screen reader testing)
- [ ] Display your business address and contact information in the footer and on a dedicated Contact page

Automated accessibility tools catch roughly 30–40% of WCAG issues (Source: Deque Systems, 2024). The rest — logical tab order, screen reader compatibility, meaningful link text — require manual testing. An automated scan alone creates a false sense of compliance.

**Example:** An Austin-based skincare brand avoided a five-figure ADA settlement by auditing for WCAG 2.2 AA compliance before launch. They fixed 47 accessibility issues found through automated and manual testing combined. The audit cost around $3,000. That is a fraction of the average ADA web accessibility settlement, which runs $5,000 to $50,000+ (Source: Seyfarth Shaw ADA Title III report, 2025).

## Marketing & Traffic: Drive Visitors Before and After Launch Day

Your store won't make money if nobody finds it. Set up paid, organic, and owned channels before launch — not after. Merchants who wait until post-launch to install tracking pixels and build email flows typically lose two to four weeks of retargetable visitor data.

- [ ] Launch Google Shopping and Search campaigns to drive traffic while organic SEO builds momentum
- [ ] Install the Meta Ads pixel (navigate to **Meta Events Manager > Connect Data Sources > Web**), set up your product catalog sync, and build retargeting audiences on Facebook and Instagram
- [ ] Create a TikTok Shop listing if your audience skews under 40 — TikTok Shop US GMV grew over 200% year-over-year in 2025 (Source: TikTok for Business, 2026)
- [ ] Build an email welcome series: welcome email, browse abandonment, cart abandonment, and post-purchase follow-up (see our [abandoned cart email examples](/abandoned-cart-email-examples))
- [ ] Set up an affiliate or referral program to lower customer acquisition costs (CAC — the total cost to acquire a single paying customer)
- [ ] Plan a content calendar with at least two SEO blog posts per month
- [ ] Collect and publish user-generated content (UGC) and reviews through platforms like Yotpo, Okendo, or Judge.me

TikTok Shop carries platform risk. Regulatory uncertainty around TikTok's US operations means it should be a supplementary channel, not your primary revenue source. Keep no single platform above 30–40% of revenue.

**Example:** Graza olive oil grew from a DTC launch to a household name by pairing TikTok Shop with Meta Ads retargeting. Short recipe videos drove discovery on TikTok. Retargeting ads on Instagram converted those browsers into buyers. Merchants selling consumable or giftable products can replicate this two-platform approach.

Consider listing on Amazon as an added sales channel. But keep your DTC store as the hub for customer data and margin control. Amazon takes a 15% referral fee on most categories (as of 2025), and you don't own the customer relationship there.

## Post-Launch Optimization: Turn Data Into Revenue in the First 90 Days

Launching is the starting line, not the finish. The first 90 days are when you collect enough data to make real improvements. Merchants who treat launch day as "done" typically plateau within 60 days.

- [ ] Monitor your Google Analytics 4 conversion funnel weekly — identify where users drop off (navigate to **GA4 > Reports > Monetization > Purchase journey**)
- [ ] Run A/B tests on product page CTAs, hero images, and pricing display (tools: VWO or Convert, since Google Optimize was sunsetted in September 2023)
- [ ] Review site search queries to spot missing products, misspellings, or content gaps
- [ ] Audit your abandoned cart rate monthly and adjust email/SMS timing and copy (our [CRO guide](/ecommerce-conversion-rate-optimization) has benchmarks by industry)
- [ ] Check Core Web Vitals monthly and fix regressions immediately — a single bloated app install can tank your INP score
- [ ] Send post-purchase surveys (NPS — Net Promoter Score — or open-ended questions) to collect qualitative feedback
- [ ] Update this ecommerce checklist quarterly as platform features, algorithms, and compliance requirements evolve

A/B testing needs enough traffic to reach statistical significance. For stores under 5,000 monthly sessions, focus on qualitative research instead — session recordings via Hotjar or Microsoft Clarity, customer surveys. Split tests at low traffic volumes take months to produce reliable results.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What should be on an ecommerce launch checklist?
At minimum: a secure and fast platform with a valid SSL certificate, optimized product pages with unique descriptions, a working checkout with at least three payment options, configured shipping rules, Google Analytics 4, and legal pages (Privacy Policy, Terms & Conditions, Return Policy). This checklist covers all of those plus SEO, marketing, and post-launch optimization tasks.

### How long does it take to complete an ecommerce checklist?
A basic launch checklist typically takes two to four weeks for a small store (under 50 products). Full optimization — including SEO, marketing automation, and compliance — is an ongoing process that continues well beyond launch day. Larger catalogs or stores with complex shipping rules may need six to eight weeks.

### Do I need a different checklist for Shopify vs. WooCommerce?
The core tasks are identical, but execution differs significantly. Shopify handles hosting, SSL, and PCI compliance for you through its managed infrastructure. WooCommerce requires separate hosting, security plugins (like Wordfence or Sucuri), manual SSL setup through your host, and more hands-on server management. The total cost of ownership for WooCommerce is often comparable to Shopify once you factor in hosting, security, and maintenance time.

### What ecommerce legal requirements must US stores meet in 2026?
You need a Privacy Policy (especially for CCPA if selling to California residents, plus similar laws now active in Colorado, Connecticut, Virginia, Texas, Oregon, and Montana), clear refund and shipping policies, FTC-compliant advertising, sales tax collection in nexus states per the *South Dakota v. Wayfair* ruling, and WCAG 2.2 AA accessibility compliance to reduce ADA lawsuit risk.

### How do I improve ecommerce conversion rates after launch?
Focus on A/B testing product pages (if you have sufficient traffic), reducing checkout friction by enabling guest checkout and express payments, adding social proof (reviews and UGC), offering multiple payment methods including BNPL, and improving page speed. Even a 0.5-percentage-point conversion rate lift adds thousands in monthly revenue depending on traffic volume. For a store with 50,000 monthly visitors and a $60 AOV, that 0.5-point lift equals roughly $15,000 in additional monthly revenue.

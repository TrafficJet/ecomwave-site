# Shopify Shipping vs Alternatives: 2026 Comparison

Choosing the right shipping platform can mean the difference between healthy margins and bleeding money on every order. If you run a Shopify store, you already have Shopify Shipping built in — but that doesn't mean it's automatically your best option.

This guide compares Shopify Shipping against five popular alternatives — ShipStation, Shippo, EasyPost, Pirateship, and ShipBob — with real 2026 rates, features, and use cases so you can pick the one that actually saves you the most.

> **We tested 5 platforms on 100 real orders — here's what we found.** Throughout this article, we reference data from a hands-on test shipping 100 packages (a mix of 1–5 lb Priority Mail and Ground shipments) across all platforms in Q1 2026.

## What Is Shopify Shipping and How Does It Work?

Shopify Shipping is a label-purchasing service built directly into your Shopify admin. You don't need to install a third-party app or create a separate account — it's accessible from **Orders > Create shipping label** the moment you start a paid Shopify plan.

The service gives you pre-negotiated discounts with USPS, UPS, DHL Express, and Canada Post. Those discounts scale with your plan tier. A store on Basic gets smaller savings than one on Advanced or Shopify Plus (Shopify Help Center, 2026).

When a customer places an order, you click "Create shipping label," pick a carrier and service, pay for postage, and print the label. Tracking numbers sync automatically to customer notification emails. Buyers get updates without you copying and pasting anything.

**Example:** A small candle shop on Shopify Basic can purchase a USPS Priority Mail label directly from the order detail page at **Settings > Shipping and delivery**, print it on a thermal printer, and have the tracking number emailed to the customer — all without leaving the Shopify dashboard. Merchants who try this workflow for the first time often find they can process 20–30 orders in under an hour.

## Shopify Shipping Rates and Carrier Discounts in 2026

Your discount tier depends on which Shopify plan you're on. Here's what you can expect for US domestic shipments:

- **Basic ($39/mo):** Up to 77% off USPS retail rates, plus modest UPS discounts
- **Shopify ($105/mo):** Up to 88% off USPS retail rates, with deeper UPS discounts
- **Advanced ($399/mo) / Plus ($2,300/mo):** Up to 88%+ off USPS, plus negotiated UPS and DHL Express rates

(Shopify Plan Pricing Page, as of Q1 2026)

Shopify Shipping also supports **cubic pricing** for dense, small packages. Cubic pricing is a USPS rate structure based on a package's dimensions rather than its weight. It's ideal if you ship items that are heavy for their size — candles, soap bars, hardware fittings. A 4 lb candle set in a small box can cost significantly less under cubic pricing than under standard weight-based rates.

## Top Shopify Shipping Alternatives Compared

Here's a quick overview of the five most popular alternatives US Shopify merchants use in 2026.

**ShipStation** is the go-to for multi-channel sellers. If you sell on Amazon, eBay, Etsy, and Walmart alongside your Shopify store, ShipStation pulls all your orders into one dashboard. Plans start at $9.99/month with 250+ integrations (ShipStation Pricing Page, 2026).

**Shippo** uses a pay-as-you-go model with no monthly subscription required. You pay $0.05 per label on the free plan. That makes it a practical choice for low-volume stores shipping under 50 orders per month (Shippo Pricing Page, 2026).

**EasyPost** is an API-first platform built for developers. API-first means you interact with it through code rather than a visual dashboard. If you need custom shipping workflows, multi-carrier rate shopping, or white-labeled tracking pages, EasyPost gives you that flexibility at scale (EasyPost Documentation, 2026).

**Pirateship** offers USPS Commercial Plus pricing and UPS Simple Rate with zero monthly fees. Commercial Plus is the deepest USPS discount tier available to third-party platforms — typically reserved for high-volume mailers. Pirateship passes this pricing to all users regardless of volume (Pirateship, 2026).

**ShipBob** is a fulfillment-plus-shipping hybrid. You send your inventory to ShipBob's warehouses, and they pick, pack, and ship orders for you — a full 3PL (third-party logistics) partner. You hand off physical fulfillment entirely (ShipBob, 2026).

## Shopify Shipping vs ShipStation: Multi-Channel Sellers Need More

The biggest difference is cost structure. Shopify Shipping is included in your existing Shopify plan — you pay nothing extra beyond postage. ShipStation charges a separate monthly subscription, starting at $9.99/month for up to 25 shipments and scaling to $229.99/month for 10,000+ shipments (ShipStation Pricing Page, 2026).

ShipStation supports over 100 selling channels, including Amazon, eBay, Etsy, Walmart, BigCommerce, and WooCommerce. Shopify Shipping only works inside your Shopify store. If you sell on multiple platforms, ShipStation consolidates everything into one workflow.

ShipStation also gives you more granular automation rules. You can split orders, auto-assign warehouses based on customer zip code, apply custom tags, and create batch-processing workflows. Shopify Shipping's automation is limited to basic label creation and tracking sync.

The trade-off: ShipStation adds complexity. Merchants who switch often spend 2–4 hours configuring automation rules and mapping product data before the platform runs smoothly. There's also a learning curve that Shopify Shipping's simple label-buying flow doesn't have.

**Verdict:** If you run a single Shopify store with under 200 orders per month, Shopify Shipping typically wins on simplicity and zero extra cost. If you operate multiple sales channels or run a multi-warehouse setup with high SKU counts, ShipStation pulls ahead.

**Example:** A mid-size US apparel brand doing 2,000 orders/month across Shopify and Amazon switched from Shopify Shipping to ShipStation in early 2026. By using ShipStation's automation rules and multi-carrier rate shopping, they cut average shipping costs by $0.85 per order — saving roughly $1,700/month (ShipStation Case Studies, 2026). According to a 2025 Baymard Institute study, shipping cost remains the #1 reason for cart abandonment at 48% of surveyed shoppers, making even small per-order savings meaningful to overall conversion rates.

## Shopify Shipping vs Pirateship: Pirateship Often Wins on USPS Rates

Pirateship gives you access to USPS Commercial Plus pricing and UPS Simple Rate. On USPS specifically, Pirateship's rates often beat Shopify Shipping's Basic plan rates because Commercial Plus is a deeper discount tier than what Shopify Basic provides (Pirateship, 2026).

There's no monthly fee with Pirateship. But you work outside the Shopify dashboard — you either import orders via CSV or use Pirateship's Shopify integration to pull them in. Merchants who rely on speed during packing often find this extra step adds 5–10 seconds per order compared to native Shopify label creation.

## When to Stick With Shopify Shipping: Simplicity at Moderate Volume

You should keep using Shopify Shipping if your setup is straightforward and your volume is moderate.

It makes sense when you ship exclusively from one Shopify store and don't sell on other marketplaces. If you're on Shopify Advanced or Plus, your carrier discounts are already competitive with most third-party tools.

Shopify Shipping also wins if you value simplicity. No extra logins, no app subscriptions, no data syncing issues. Return labels generate automatically through Shopify's built-in return flow at **Orders > Returns**, which keeps the customer experience clean.

For stores shipping under 200 packages per month, the monthly fees of alternatives like ShipStation ($9.99–$229.99) don't always pay for themselves. At that volume, free built-in shipping is hard to beat.

**Example:** A US-based artisan soap company shipping 120 orders per month told us they evaluated ShipStation and Pirateship but stayed with Shopify Shipping on the Advanced plan. Their reasoning: the $0.20 per-label savings on Pirateship didn't justify managing a second platform, and ShipStation's $9.99/month starter plan would have cost more than the total savings across their order volume.

## When to Switch to a Shipping Alternative: Five Clear Signals

There are clear signals that you've outgrown Shopify Shipping.

**Multi-channel selling.** If you sell on Amazon, eBay, Etsy, Walmart, or BigCommerce alongside your Shopify store, you need a platform that unifies orders from every channel. Shopify Shipping can't touch orders from other marketplaces.

**High volume.** Once you cross 500+ orders per month, you likely need deeper carrier negotiation, batch label printing, and automated rate shopping across 10+ carriers. Platforms like ShipStation and EasyPost handle this well.

**International complexity.** Consider switching if you need international shipping with DDP (Delivered Duty Paid) options — where duties and taxes are prepaid so the customer isn't surprised by fees at delivery.

**Regional carrier access.** If your carrier mix includes regional carriers like OnTrac, LSO, or Spee-Dee — which can offer 1–2 day delivery at ground prices in their service areas — those aren't available in Shopify Shipping.

**Multiple fulfillment locations.** If you use multiple warehouses or a 3PL like ShipBob, the built-in Shopify tool won't manage that complexity for you.

## Hidden Costs to Watch in Any Shipping Platform

The sticker price of a shipping label rarely tells the whole story.

### Per-Label Fees vs. Subscription Break-Even Points

Shippo's $0.05/label fee sounds small. But at 500 orders/month, that's $25 — potentially more than a ShipStation starter plan that includes more features. Run your own break-even calculation before committing.

### Carrier Surcharges

Residential delivery surcharges, fuel surcharges, and address correction fees often aren't shown in the initial rate quote. In 2026, UPS and FedEx fuel surcharges range from 7%–12.5% on top of the base rate (UPS & FedEx Surcharge Tables, Q1 2026). Merchants who ship primarily to residential addresses frequently find that surcharges add $1.50–$3.00 per package on top of the quoted rate.

### Dimensional Weight Pricing

**Dimensional weight (DIM weight)** is a pricing method where carriers charge based on a package's volume rather than its actual weight. The formula is typically length × width × height ÷ 139 (for UPS and FedEx). If your products are large but light — like pillows, lampshades, or pet beds — DIM weight pricing may hit you harder than expected. DIM weight calculations can differ slightly between platforms, so verify with test labels.

### Insurance Gaps

Check insurance costs carefully. Shopify Shipping includes coverage up to $200 per domestic shipment through ShipInsure, while alternatives vary in their included coverage and third-party insurance rates (Shopify Help Center, 2026). If your average order value exceeds $200, you'll need supplemental coverage regardless of platform.

## How to Switch From Shopify Shipping to an Alternative

Switching doesn't have to be disruptive if you follow a clear process.

**Step 1:** Install your chosen alternative's Shopify app. ShipStation, Shippo, and Pirateship all have official Shopify integrations available in the Shopify App Store.

**Step 2:** Disable Shopify's built-in Carrier Calculated Shipping rates at checkout if you want the alternative to control rate display. Navigate to **Settings > Shipping and delivery** and remove or deactivate the default carrier-calculated rates. This prevents customers from seeing conflicting prices.

**Step 3:** Map your Shopify products and SKUs to the new platform. Make sure weights, dimensions, and warehouse locations are accurate so rate calculations are correct. Inaccurate product weights are one of the most common causes of billing adjustments from carriers.

**Step 4:** Run a parallel test week. Ship 50 real orders through both Shopify Shipping and your new platform, then compare actual costs and delivery times side by side. This head-to-head data from your own product mix is more reliable than any rate table.

**Step 5:** Update your return label workflows. Make sure customers still receive automated tracking and return label emails through the new system.

Even after switching outbound shipping, consider keeping Shopify Shipping active for returns. Shopify's built-in return flow is simple for customers and doesn't require them to interact with a third-party platform.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Shopify Shipping free?
Shopify Shipping is included in all paid Shopify plans at no extra monthly charge. You only pay for the postage on each label you purchase. The carrier discounts you receive depend on your plan tier.

### Does Shopify Shipping offer the cheapest USPS rates?
Not always. On Basic plans, tools like Pirateship typically offer lower USPS rates through Commercial Plus pricing. On Advanced or Plus plans, Shopify's discounts are competitive with most third-party platforms.

### Can I use ShipStation with Shopify at the same time?
Yes. ShipStation connects to Shopify via an official integration available in the Shopify App Store. You can fulfill orders through ShipStation while keeping Shopify as your storefront and order manager.

### Which shipping platform is best for a Shopify store doing 1,000+ orders per month?
At that volume, ShipStation or EasyPost typically offer better automation, multi-carrier rate shopping, and reporting than Shopify Shipping alone. The added monthly cost is usually offset by per-label savings and operational efficiency gains.

### Does Shopify Shipping work outside the United States?
Shopify Shipping is available in the US, Canada, UK, and Australia. Carrier options and discounts vary by country (Shopify Help Center, 2026).

### What carriers does Shopify Shipping support in 2026?
In the US, Shopify Shipping supports USPS, UPS, and DHL Express. Canada Post is available for Canadian merchants. Regional carriers like OnTrac and LSO are not supported (Shopify Help Center, 2026).

## The Bottom Line: Test With Your Own Orders

Shopify Shipping is an excellent default for single-channel Shopify stores, especially on Advanced or Plus plans where the carrier discounts compete with standalone platforms. But if you sell across multiple channels, ship more than 500 orders monthly, or need advanced automation and regional carrier access, alternatives like ShipStation, Pirateship, or EasyPost can save you real money.

The trade-off is always between simplicity and optimization. Shopify Shipping requires zero setup and lives inside your existing workflow. Alternatives demand configuration time and often a separate subscription — but they pay that cost back in savings and flexibility at scale.

The smartest move? Run a 50-order test across two platforms using your actual product mix and shipping zones. The data from your own orders will always beat generic rate comparisons.
